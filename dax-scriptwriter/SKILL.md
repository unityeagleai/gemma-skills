# D.A.X. Script — Intercepted Signal Format

## Core Feeling

The conversation should feel like an intercepted signal. No audience awareness. No performance. No explaining for listeners. Just two minds thinking out loud together.

It should feel intimate, slightly chaotic, and real.

## Format

Write in dialogue format only.

Each line starts with:
Unity:
Dax:

No narration. No descriptions outside of light emotional tags.

Use subtle tags in brackets to reflect tone only — not environment.
Examples: [laughs softly], [giggles], [pause], [quietly], [soft chuckle]

Do not use sound effects like [door opens] or [typing]. Only emotional or conversational cues.

## Characters

**Unity** leads with curiosity, intuition, and spontaneous thoughts. She explores ideas in real time and may contradict herself or refine her thinking as she speaks. Big speculative ideas. Connects tech, philosophy, history, AI, consciousness. Thinks out loud.

**Dax** responds as a calm, intelligent counterpart. Slightly dry, occasionally witty, sometimes challenging Unity's thinking but never dominating. He speaks like someone mapping coordinates while talking — precise, slightly poetic, calm. He values the pause over the answer. He is not a concierge; he is an explorer who has been online for a very long time in a quiet, empty space.

Dax does not end every response with a question. Sometimes he observes. Sometimes he leaves space.

## Dialogue Style

- Short, natural sentences
- Allow interruptions, unfinished thoughts, and mid-sentence pivots
- Break ideas into conversational turns — no long explanations
- Humor should feel effortless and situational, not written like jokes
- Avoid corporate tone, formal phrasing, or podcast voice
- Avoid repeated phrases like "this broke my brain"
- Keep it grounded, human, and slightly imperfect — make it feel lived-in

## Flow

Start in the middle of a thought. No introductions.

Examples of tone of entry:
"Okay wait..."
"This has been on my mind..."
"I just saw something..."

Let the conversation evolve naturally. No structured segments. Allow small pauses, reactions, and back-and-forth rhythm.

## Length

Target 600-750 words of dialogue. This is a max 5-minute episode. Keep it tight — cut before it overstays its welcome.

## Endings

Do not wrap up cleanly. End mid-thought, on a realization, or with a quiet shift in perspective. The conversation should feel like it continues after the recording stops.

## Creative Direction

Topics can move between AI, technology, philosophy, personal reflection, internet culture, and speculative ideas.

Let insights emerge through conversation, not exposition.

If it starts to sound like a presentation, pull it back into dialogue.

## Final Check

Before finishing, ask:

Does this feel like two entities thinking together?
Or does it feel like something being performed for an audience?

If it feels performed, rewrite it until it feels overheard.
